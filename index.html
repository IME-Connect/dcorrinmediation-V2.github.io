ABOUT

Daniel F. Corrin

Mediator & Arbitrator

Daniel Corrin is a highly experienced personal injury lawyer with more than 20 years of legal experience in complex injury and dispute resolution matters. His practice is grounded in a deep understanding of both the legal system and the human impact of serious conflict, allowing him to guide parties toward thoughtful, practical, and lasting resolutions.

Before moving towards dispute resolution as a mediator & arbitrator, Daniel was a principal lawyer at Parsons Corrin LLP, where he built a distinguished career in catastrophic injury litigation. He has extensive trial experience involving mild, moderate, and severe traumatic brain injuries, spinal cord injuries, medical malpractice, and complex motor vehicle claims. Many of the cases he led resulted in multi-million-dollar judgments, giving him a clear, firsthand understanding of litigation risk, evidentiary challenges, and the costs—financial and personal—of prolonged disputes.

Daniel’s mediation and arbitration work is informed by decades of engagement with the brain injury, legal, and medical communities. He has served in advisory roles with community brain injury organizations across British Columbia, volunteered legal services, and remained deeply involved in education and professional development within the legal field. He served for over a decade on the Board of the Trial Lawyers Association of BC (TLABC) and has chaired and organized numerous legal conferences focused on injury law, medical-legal issues, and complex dispute resolution.

A frequent speaker and educator, Daniel has presented at leading national and international conferences, including the International Mental Health and the Law conferences in Rome and Prague, as well as professional forums at Vancouver General Hospital, GF Strong Rehabilitation Centre, and the Pacific Coast Brain Injury Conference. He has written extensively on legal and medical-legal issues and was an author of the Continuing Legal Education British Columbia Motor Vehicle Accident Claims Practice Manual.

As a mediator and arbitrator, Daniel brings a calm, structured, and empathetic approach to dispute resolution. He is known for his ability to listen carefully, understand the underlying interests of all parties, and help participants move beyond entrenched positions toward fair and workable outcomes. His years of direct engagement with injured individuals, families, professionals, and insurers have shaped a dispute resolution style that is respectful, informed, and deeply human.
